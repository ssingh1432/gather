Community Guidelines

Effective date: July 20, 2026

Gather exists to help communities connect. These Guidelines explain, in plain language, what that looks like day to day. They work alongside our Terms of Service — if something's against the Terms, it's against these Guidelines too.

Be real

Use your own identity. Don't impersonate someone else or pretend to be an official account you're not. Don't run networks of fake accounts to inflate likes, followers, or views.

Be respectful

Disagreement is fine; targeted harassment isn't. Don't post hate speech, threats, doxxing (sharing someone's private information without consent), or content designed to demean people based on who they are.

Keep it safe

No content that sexualizes or endangers minors, promotes self-harm, incites violence, or supports terrorism or violent extremism — these are removed on sight and may be reported to authorities.

Post content you have the right to post

Don't upload content that infringes someone else's copyright or trademark. See our Copyright & IP Policy if you believe your work was posted without permission.

No spam or manipulation

Don't flood the Platform with repetitive or unsolicited content, use bots to automate engagement, or try to game feeds, search, or the monetization program.

Respect privacy

Don't post someone else's private information (address, phone number, private messages, identity documents) without their consent, and don't use Gather to stalk or monitor someone.

What happens if you break these rules

Depending on severity and history, we may remove content, restrict features, suspend, or permanently ban an account. If action is taken against you, you can appeal from Settings → Grievances & legal requests. Repeat violations, especially of intellectual property or safety rules, are treated more seriously and can lead to permanent termination.

How to report something

Use the report option on any post, comment, or profile for quick community-standards reports. For matters that need formal legal review — illegal content, privacy violations, defamation, impersonation — use "File a complaint" from the report screen or from Settings → Grievances & legal requests.
