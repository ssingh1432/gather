Copyright & Intellectual Property Policy

Effective date: July 20, 2026

Gather respects intellectual property rights and expects users to do the same. This page explains how to report content that infringes your copyright, trademark, or other IP rights, and how we handle those reports.

This is a reporting and takedown process, not a substitute for legal advice. If you're unsure whether something infringes your rights, consider consulting a lawyer.

How to report infringing content

The most reliable way to report copyright or IP infringement is directly in the app: open the post or profile, tap Report, then "This is illegal content or a privacy violation," and choose the relevant complaint type. This routes your report to our legal review queue with a documented trail. You can also email legal@eiquoab.xyz with:

  • A description of the copyrighted work or IP right you believe is infringed.
  • A link (or clear description) of the content on Gather you're reporting.
  • Your contact information.
  • A statement that you have a good-faith belief the use is unauthorized, and that the information in your report is accurate.

What happens after you report

We review reports and, where warranted, remove or restrict access to the reported content and log the takedown against the complaint, including the legal basis relied on. The account responsible is notified and may submit an appeal (Settings → Grievances & legal requests) if they believe the removal was made in error.

Counter-notice

If your content was removed and you believe this was a mistake or misidentification, you can appeal through Settings → Grievances & legal requests, or reply to our notification email, explaining why you believe the content doesn't infringe. We review appeals and may reinstate the content.

Repeat infringers

Accounts that repeatedly infringe others' intellectual property rights may be permanently terminated, consistent with our Terms of Service.

False reports

Don't file a report you know to be false or misleading — doing so may itself violate our Terms and, depending on the circumstances, applicable law.
