Law Enforcement Request Policy

Effective date: July 20, 2026

This page explains how Gather handles requests from law enforcement, courts, and other government authorities for information about our users, consistent with the Electronic Transactions Act, 2063, the Privacy Act, 2075, and applicable Nepali legal process.

How to submit a request

Government and law enforcement requests should be sent to legal@eiquoab.xyz on official letterhead, and should include:

  • The requesting authority and a verifiable official contact.
  • The legal basis for the request (e.g. court order, warrant, or statutory authority).
  • The specific account(s) or content involved and the specific data requested.
  • A reference/case number, where applicable.

We log every request we receive, regardless of outcome, in our internal legal data request register for accountability.

How we evaluate requests

We review each request for legal sufficiency before responding. We may seek clarification, narrow an overly broad request, or decline to comply where we believe a request lacks proper legal basis, is overbroad, or is inconsistent with applicable law. We do not guarantee compliance with every request we receive.

Emergency requests

Where there is a good-faith belief of imminent risk of death or serious physical harm to a person, we may voluntarily disclose limited information necessary to prevent that harm, consistent with applicable law, even absent formal legal process.

User notification

Where legally permitted, we aim to notify the affected user that their information was requested. We may delay or withhold notice where required by law, a valid non-disclosure order, or where notice would risk an ongoing investigation or someone's safety.

Preservation requests

We can preserve account data in connection with an active investigation for a limited period upon a valid request, pending receipt of formal legal process for disclosure.
