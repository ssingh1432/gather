Privacy Policy

Effective date: July 20, 2026  •  Version: 2026-07-20

Gather ("Gather", "we", "us", "our") operates the social platform available at eiquoab.xyz and through the Gather mobile and web apps (together, the "Platform"). This Policy explains what personal data we collect, why, how we use it, who we share it with, and the choices and rights you have — including the rights available to you under Nepal's Privacy Act, 2075 and the Electronic Transactions Act, 2063, and comparable rights we extend to users elsewhere.

Gather is built on Supabase (PostgreSQL database, authentication, file storage, realtime, and serverless functions). We describe below every other service that processes your data on our behalf.

1. Legal basis for processing

We rely on one or more of the following legal bases, depending on the activity:

  • Consent — for account creation, optional profile fields, marketing communications, and any processing where we ask you to opt in. You accept this Policy and our Terms of Service when you sign up; the app records the version and date you accepted so you and we both have a record of it.
  • Contract — processing needed to create your account, operate your feed, deliver notifications, and provide the features you use.
  • Legal obligation — responding to lawful requests from Nepali or other authorities, and complying with recordkeeping obligations.
  • Legitimate interests — securing the Platform (fraud and abuse prevention, rate limiting, audit logging), improving reliability, and enforcing our Terms — balanced against your right to privacy.

2. Information we collect

2.1 Information you provide

  • Account & profile: username, email address, password (stored as a salted hash by Supabase Auth — we never see or store your plaintext password), optional phone number for SMS verification, display name, bio, profile photo, cover photo.
  • Content: posts, comments/replies, quote posts, reactions and likes, bookmarks, stories, tags, location tags you choose to add, and any photos, videos, or audio you upload.
  • Social graph: who you follow, your followers, close friends list, blocked accounts, and muted accounts.
  • Reports and complaints: content reports you file, formal legal complaints or appeals you submit, and any evidence you attach to them.
  • Creator/payout information: if you opt into the creator monetization program, the payout details and preferences you provide.
  • Identity verification: if you request account verification, the document type and image you submit for review.
  • Support communications: anything you send us directly.

2.2 Information collected automatically

  • Device & push token: a Firebase Cloud Messaging token so we can deliver push notifications to your device.
  • Security & audit data: login attempts, IP address, device/browser information, and security-relevant events (e.g. password changes, new device sign-ins) — kept in a security audit log used to detect brute-force attempts and abuse.
  • Usage analytics: in-app interactions (e.g. views, taps) recorded in our own first-party analytics tables to understand feature usage and improve the Platform. We do not use a third-party analytics SDK.
  • Approximate location: only if you choose to tag a location on a post, or if you grant the app location permission for that feature — never collected silently in the background.

2.3 Information from third parties

We do not currently offer "Sign in with Google/Apple" — accounts are created directly with an email/password or phone number. If we add third-party sign-in in the future, this Policy will be updated before it launches. Publicly posted content from other users (e.g. someone tagging or mentioning you) may reference your account.

4. How we use your information

  • Create, secure, and maintain your account.
  • Operate your feed, notifications, search, and social features.
  • Detect and prevent fraud, spam, and abuse; enforce rate limits and brute-force protection.
  • Respond to reports, legal complaints, and appeals, and maintain the audit trail those require.
  • Run the creator monetization/ads program, including calculating payouts.
  • Understand how features are used, through our own first-party analytics, so we can improve the Platform.
  • Communicate with you about your account, security, and (where you've opted in) product updates.
  • Comply with legal obligations, including requests from Nepali authorities under the Electronic Transactions Act, 2063, and equivalent lawful requests from other jurisdictions.

5. AI and automated processing

Gather does not currently use AI/machine-learning models to generate your feed, rank content, moderate content, or process your personal data. Ranking and recommendation features today are rule-based (for example, showing content from accounts you follow, or surfacing accounts you may know based on your existing connections). If we introduce AI-powered features that use your data — for recommendations, moderation, or otherwise — we will update this section to explain what data is used, how, and what choices you have, before that feature launches.

6. Cookies and similar technologies

The Gather web app uses browser storage (not always traditional cookies) in these categories:

  • Essential — keeps you signed in and the app functioning (session and authentication tokens). Cannot be disabled without breaking the app.
  • Functional — remembers preferences like your privacy settings.
  • Analytics — our own first-party, in-app usage analytics described above.
  • Advertising — Google AdMob may set identifiers to serve and measure ads; see our Cookie Policy for how to opt out on your device.

7. Data security

We use industry-standard safeguards, including encrypted connections (HTTPS everywhere), encryption of sensitive data at rest, row-level security policies on every database table, rate limiting and brute-force protection on authentication, and a security audit log. No system is completely secure, and we can't guarantee absolute security — please use a strong, unique password and report any suspected compromise to us immediately.

8. Your rights and choices

From the app (Settings → Data & Privacy), you can:

  • Access & export your data — request a downloadable copy of your profile, posts, comments, follows, notifications, consent history, and more.
  • Correct your profile information at any time.
  • Delete your account — see Section 9 below.
  • Control visibility — who can find you in search, see your last-seen status, message you, or tag you.
  • Block or mute other accounts.
  • Review and withdraw consent — see your consent history and update your decision.
  • Object or restrict certain processing, and file a formal privacy complaint through Settings → Grievances & legal requests.

For anything not self-serviceable in the app, contact us at privacy@eiquoab.xyz. We verify your identity before acting on a request and respond as promptly as we reasonably can.

9. Account deletion: what's removed, what's retained

Requesting deletion (Settings → Data & Privacy → Delete my account) schedules permanent deletion after a 14-day grace period, which you can cancel at any time before it completes. Once the grace period ends:

  • Permanently deleted: your account and login credentials, profile, posts, comments, likes, bookmarks, stories, follow relationships, notifications, and other data directly tied to your account, through our database's cascading deletion rules.
  • Retained where legally or operationally necessary: records of legal complaints or reports involving your account may be retained with the personal identifier removed, for our recordkeeping obligations and to preserve the integrity of other users' active complaints; security audit log entries may be retained for a limited period for fraud/abuse investigation; content you posted that another user has legitimately saved, screenshotted, or that has already been shared is outside our control and won't be removed retroactively.

10. Data retention

We keep personal data only as long as needed for the purposes in this Policy, or as required by law. Consent records are kept indefinitely as an append-only history (each new decision adds a record rather than overwriting the last) so we always have accurate proof of what was agreed to and when.

11. Children's privacy

Gather is not directed at children, and you must be at least 13 years old to create an account. If you become aware that a child under 13 has provided us personal data, please contact privacy@eiquoab.xyz and we will take steps to remove it.

12. International data transfers

Your data may be processed in Nepal and in other countries where our service providers operate (see Section 3). Where data is transferred outside Nepal, we rely on our providers' own security and contractual safeguards to protect it.

13. Copyright and intellectual property reports

If you believe content on Gather infringes your copyright or other intellectual property rights, see our Copyright & IP Policy for how to submit a report.

14. Law enforcement and government requests

See our Law Enforcement Request Policy for how we evaluate and respond to requests for user data from government or law enforcement bodies.

15. Changes to this Policy

We may update this Policy from time to time. Material changes will be announced in the app, and you may be asked to review and accept the new version — each acceptance is recorded against the specific policy version, so we always know which version you agreed to.
